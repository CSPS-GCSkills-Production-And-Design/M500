What is Delegation of Authority?

Before proceeding further, please watch this short video on delegation of authority as defined in the Financial Administration Act. It will provide the backdrop to the details that will be presented to you.

As you saw in the video, ministers and deputy heads are given responsibility for the control and spending of public funds within their departments. Deputy heads (DHs) are responsible for establishing procedures to control and account for financial commitments and maintain the records pertaining to them. Departmental chief financial officers support DHs in managing the delegated authorities within their departments and ensuring that the following authorities are delegated in writing by the minister or DH to positions identified by title, not to individuals identified by name.

Ministers and deputy heads sub‑delegate their authorities, in whole or in part, to assistant deputy ministers, directors general, directors, cost centre managers, and other officials as required. The exact positions or levels to whom delegation of authority is granted, the dollar limits, and the area of authority of each are specified in the department’s Delegation of Spending and Financial Signing Authorities Chart. Note that the look, format, and contents of a chart can vary from department to department.

Here’s a sample of a Delegation of Spending and Financial Signing Authorities Chart. Please note that this partial delegation of authorities chart for a fictitious department has been developed strictly for the purposes of this lesson. It does not necessarily contain all of the elements and information that would normally be included in a complete or actual chart. Consult your departmental delegation of authorities chart and related documentation in order to obtain the complete and exact delegation information for your department.

This sample chart distinguishes the various departmental levels and their financial and transaction authorities. Contracting officers and other public servants with delegation of authority to contract for goods and services are responsible for doing so up to the specified dollar value sub‑delegated to them. Notice the various types and levels of authorities that can be sub‑delegated. Contracting officers and other public servants are responsible for ensuring that they clearly understand what authority has been sub‑delegated to them and that they work within their authority.

Whether contracting for goods, services or construction services, the established thresholds must not be exceeded.

The diagram below illustrates the source of the authority to contract for goods and services, how it is sub‑delegated within departments, and how it must be exercised.

Graphic description

Diagram on the authority to enter into contracts for goods and services

Description:

The diagram illustrates the inherent power ministers have in regards to contracting for goods and services as identified in each Department’s establishing act.

Hereunder are two streams: Authority to enter into contracts for services and Authority to enter into contracts for goods.

  1. The Authority to enter into contracts for services is delegated to ministers through their organization’s enabling legislations and then sub‑delegated to departmental officials through the organizational delegation instrument and up to the Treasury Board Contracting Limits.
  2. The Authority to enter into contracts for goods.

The authority given to the Minister of PSPC in section 9 of the Department of Public Works and Government Services Act supplants the authority of each minister to contract for goods. The authority is restored to the extent determined by the Minister of PSPC through a delegation, in accordance with section 8 of the Act.

Using a Delegation of Authority letter, the Minister of PSPC then delegates this authority to ministers to procure goods for their respective organizations according to specific delegated amount. Through their organizational delegation instruments, the authority to contract for goods is then sub‑delegated to their departmental officials.
