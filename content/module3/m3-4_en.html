Contracting Limits

No matter the source of the authority to enter into contracts, ministers cannot enter into or amend contracts that exceed the limits contained in the Treasury Board Contracts Directive (which is also known as Appendix C of the Treasury Board Contracting Policy) without first obtaining the approval of the Treasury Board.

In Appendix C – Part I of the Treasury Board Contracting Policy, you will notice in each table (schedules 1, 2 and 3) how the authority to enter into and amend contracts varies depending on the sourcing method.

Select each of the following tabs to learn more about each method.

Competitive

The authority to enter into and amend contracts is greater when using the competitive approach—which means either using an electronic tendering approach in which a notice of proposed procurement is published on the Government Electronic Tendering Service (GETS) website so that all potential suppliers can see the opportunity and submit a bid OR using a traditional approach by inviting selected suppliers to submit a bid.

Non‑competitive

The authority to enter into and amend contracts decreases when a non‑competitive approach is used—awarding a sole-source contract.


Schedule 5 of the Treasury Board Contracts Directive (Appendix C of the TB Contracting Policy) details the requirements for services contracts with former public servants (FPS) in receipt of a pension under the Public Service Superannuation Act (PSSA).Competitive and non-competitive service contracts with former public servants in receipt of a pension are subject to the minister’s basic contracting limits. However, the department’s responsible minister must provide contract approval prior to entering into a non-competitive service contract with a former public servant in receipt of a pension when:

  • the FPS is in receipt of pension; AND the contract is over $200,000 
    OR
  • the annualized rate of remuneration exceeds $200,000 and the contract term exceeds ninety (90) working days
    OR
  • the FPS is in receipt of lump sum payments when total fees payable exceed $5,000 during the lump sum payment period.

Finally, the Treasury Board Contracts Directive also provides other departmental exceptional contracting limits and emergency contracting limits.

Select each element to view some situations in which exceptional and emergency contracting limits could apply.

Exceptional contracting limits

Situation 1

The Minister of Public Services and Procurement Canada may enter into or amend a contract to repair and overhaul military equipment up to $50 million.

Situation  2

The Minister responsible for Library and Archives Canada may enter into or amend a non-competitive contract to purchase historical material up to $150,000.

Situation  3

Until October 2022, the Minister of Global Affairs Canada, for the purpose of Canadian missions abroad, may enter into and amend competitive security service contracts up to $10 million.

Situation  4

The Minister responsible for the Canada School of Public Service, for the purpose of teaching and research services related to its operations, may enter into a non‑competitive service contract with a contractor, including one with a former official of the public service in receipt of a pension under the PSSA up to $150,000.

Emergency contracting limits

Situation 1

Any contracting authority may enter into and amend a contract up to $1 million (including amendments and all applicable taxes including GST or HST) in response to a pressing emergency.

Situation  2

The Minister responsible for Global Affairs Canada may enter into a contract for an international development assistance program or project up to $4 million (including starter contracts) in response to a pressing emergency.

Situation 3

The Minister of National Defence may enter into non-competitive contracts up to $5 million in response to pressing emergencies for fuel, food, water and transportation services during urgent deployments of Canadian Forces units, under authorized operational orders, and in situations where there will be significant human and/or financial risk.

Situation  4

The Minister of Fisheries and Oceans may enter into non-competitive contracts up to $10 million in response to pressing emergencies caused by oil spills, and in situations where there will be significant human and/or financial risk.

NOTE: When any of the emergency contracting limits described in Part III of the Treasury Board Contracts Directive (Appendix C) are used, a report is to be sent to the Treasury Board Secretariat within 60 calendar days of the authorization or beginning of work. 

In conclusion, it is important to note that when the estimated dollar value of a procurement for goods or services exceeds a department’s delegated authority or the department’s contracting limits identified in the Treasury Board Contracting Limits, the department must either send a requisition to PSPC in order for PSPC to conduct the procurement on the department’s behalf, or obtain approval from Treasury Board (via a TB Submission) to conduct the procurement on its own.
