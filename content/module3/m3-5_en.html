Question 1 — Select the correct answer.

Departmental officials with delegation of authority to contract can, with the approval of the deputy minister, enter into a contract even when the estimated dollar value of the said contract exceeds the limits set out in the Treasury Board Contracts Directive.

Your answer is correct.

Your answer is incorrect.

The answer is: False.

Where the estimated dollar value of a contract exceeds the department’s limit (as identified in the Treasury Board Contracts Directive), the department must either submit a request to Public Services and Procurement Canada (PSPC) for that department to award the contract on the department’s behalf or first obtain approval from the Treasury Board (via a Treasury Board Submission) to enter into the contract on its own behalf.

Question 1 — Select the correct answer.

The Treasury Board Contracts Directive states that departments may enter into and amend a contract up to a total value of $1 million in response to a pressing emergency. A pressing emergency is defined as being a situation where an unforeseen nonlife–threatening event has occurred or when a departmental senior official requires a good or a service immediately.

Your answer is correct.

Your answer is incorrect.

The answer is: False.

A pressing emergency is defined as being an actual or imminent life-threatening situation, a disaster which endangers the quality of life or has resulted in the loss of life, or one that may result in significant loss or damage to Crown property.
