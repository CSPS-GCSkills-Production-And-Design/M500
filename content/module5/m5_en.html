Question 1 / 1 — Select the correct answer.

You have received bids that include certified cheques for the required security deposit. When should you cash/deposit the certified cheques submitted by the bidders?

Your answer is correct.

Your answer is incorrect.

The answer is: When a tender is accepted and the certified cheque is required as security until completion of the contract.

A contracting authority shall hold any bid bond, payment bond, performance bond or non-negotiable security deposit that is received by the authority until the terms of the security are fulfilled. When a tender is accepted and a certified cheque is required as security until completion of the contract, the cheque is deposited into the Consolidated Revenue Fund by an authorized person in the department’s finance division.

The Directive on Government Contracts, including Real Property Leases, in the Nunavut Settlement Area aims to:

Your answer is incorrect.

Question 1 / 1 — Select the correct answer.

As stated in Part I (Basic contracting limits) of the Contracts Directive, the limit for departments and agencies (other than PSPC, DND and SSC) to enter into competitive contracts for goods is set at $750,000. Based on that limit alone, can a department award a competitive contract for goods valued at $226,000 (including all taxes)?

Your answer is correct.

Your answer is incorrect.

The answer is: No.

Although the Treasury Board Contracts Directive’s limit to enter into competitive contracts for goods is $750,000, the standard delegation of authority for goods from the Minister of PSPC is only $25,000. In order to award a contract for goods, where the dollar value exceeds $25,000, a special delegation of authority must first be sought from the Minister of PSPC.

Question 1 / 1 — Select the correct answer.

Emergency contracting limits provide all departments and agencies with authority to enter into and amend a contract with a total value of up to $1 million (including amendments) in response to a pressing emergency.

Your answer is correct.

Your answer is incorrect.

The answer is: True.

Notwithstanding the basic and exceptional contracting limits provided in the Contracts Directive, any contracting authority may enter into and amend a contract up to a total value of $1 million (including amendments and all applicable taxes including GST or HST) in response to a pressing emergency on condition that a report be sent to the Treasury Board Secretariat within 60 calendar days of the authorization or beginning of the work.

Question 1 / 1 — Select the correct answer.

You have a requirement for the performance of legal services. Which of the following common service organizations has the authority to enter into a contract for this type of services?

Your answer is correct.

Your answer is incorrect.

The answer is: Department of Justice Canada.

The Government Contracts Regulations require that contracts for the performance of legal services may be entered into only by or under the authority of the Minister of Justice. No contracting authority may enter into a contract for services with a practicing member of the bar without first having obtained the Department of Justice’s approval, or having been advised by the Department of Justice that the contract does not involve legal services.

Your answer is incorrect.

Question 1 / 1 — Select the correct answer.

The international trade agreements require that each party have an independent bid challenge authority. Which of the following organizations is the bid challenge authority for federal government procurement covered by any of the trade agreements?

Question 1 / 1 — Select the correct answer.

The Delegation of Financial Signing Authorities Matrix is a standard document issued by PSPC for use by all departments. The delegations are exactly the same from one department to another. A person transferring from one department to another automatically has the same delegation of signing authorities in the new department.

Your answer is correct.

Your answer is incorrect.

The answer is: False.

There is no standard delegation of authority matrix document and PSPC does not issue Delegation of Financial Signing Authorities matrices. Although matrices contain the same type of information, they are not necessarily exactly the same from one department to another. In addition, a delegation of authority is not carried over from one position/job to another (one department to another). You must always consult your department’s delegation of authority matrix before signing off on any element of expenditure management.

Question 1 / 1 — Select the correct answer.

A department must seek ministerial approval if it intends to award a $210,000 sole source contract to a former public servant in receipt of a pension.

Your answer is correct.

Your answer is incorrect.

The answer is: True.

For non-competitive contracts with former public servants, minister’s approval is required when:

  • the FPS is in receipt of Pension; AND the contract is over $200,000,
    OR
  • the term is more than 90 days and the rate of pay, if extended over a year, equals more than $200,000
    OR
  • the FPS is in receipt of lump sum payments when fees exceed $5,000 during the lump sum payment period.
