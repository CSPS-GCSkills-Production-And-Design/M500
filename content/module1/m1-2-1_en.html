Clients

From the moment a decision is made to purchase goods or services or construction services, the work begins between the CO and the client. Together, they will ensure that every reasonable effort is made to meet the operational requirements, while obtaining the best value for money.

The role of the CO and the client is to ensure that adequate consideration is given to what is needed for the successful execution of the contract. Once the contract is in place, they must ensure the administration of the contract in accordance with the contract’s terms and conditions.

Responsibility Centre Managers (RCMs)

Responsibility Centre Managers (RCMs) are managers with delegated authority to manage financial, materiel, and human resources. They are responsible for determining requirements, controlling costs of operational accomplishments, and exercising spending authority (in accordance with section 32 of the Financial Administration Act [FAA]) to approve charges against their budgets. RCMs are also responsible for approving all disposals of materiel in their respective areas, in accordance with government policies.

RCMs may choose to delegate project management activities, including signing authority (in accordance with section 34 of the
FAA covering certification that work has been satisfactorily performed), to their staff. RCMs are accountable for contracting activities undertaken on their behalf by their staff.

Technical or Scientific Authorities

Technical or Scientific Authorities are those persons with the authority to make decisions with respect to technical requirements for a deliverable under a contract.

They are not contracting personnel but rather administrative, professional or technical staff, usually having a vested interest in seeing the work performed.

They are the persons best equipped to:

  • Implement the contract and oversee the work performed;
  • Brief, monitor, and interact with the contractor or consultant; and
  • Maintain documentation on the decisions and significant developments affecting the contract.

COs must not presume that technical or scientific authorities are necessarily knowledgeable about contracting.

Functional Authorities

In areas like informatics, facility management, and communications, departments usually give a specific mandate and authority to a group of experts (“Functional Authorities”) to review and approve deliverables as well as oversee changes in requirements.

Again, COs must not presume that functional authorities are necessarily knowledgeable about contracting.

Project Authorities or Project Managers or Project Sponsors

Project authorities or project managers or project sponsors are the individuals in charge of a project.

The project authority or manager or project sponsor usually takes the first steps in the contracting process. This person defines the nature and scope of the work to be done (in concert with the RCM and/or the technical or scientific authority, as required).
