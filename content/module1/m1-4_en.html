Question  — Select the correct answer.

A common service organization provides both optional and mandatory acquisition services to departments, boards and agencies of the Government of Canada in support of their program objectives. Which of the following operates as a common service organization?

Your answer is correct.

Your answer is incorrect.

The answer is: Public Services and Procurement Canada

PSPC is an optional service provider for the procurement of services such as assets management of movables, contracting for printing, publishing, and public warehousing.

However, under the Common Services Policy, PSPC is a mandatory provider of various procurement-related services such as:

  • Research and development contracting;
  • Executive vehicle acquisitions;
  • Guard services;
  • Co-ordination of, and contracting for, advertising and paid announcements;
  • Co-ordination of, and contracting for, public opinion research;
  • Shared travel services initiative;
  • Central removal; and
  • Film, video and multimedia production.

Question  — Select the correct answer.

Who am I? Whether I am called a business owner, a responsibility centre manager, a technical/scientific authority, a functional authority, a project authority, a project manager or a project sponsor, I am responsible for defining operational requirements.

Your answer is correct.

Your answer is incorrect.

The answer is: Client

Clients are responsible for defining their requirements and identifying the total life-cycle cost of the procurement to meet their operational needs.

Question  — Select the correct answer.

Which sector of PSPC provides expert assistance at each stage of the supply cycle and offers tools that simplify and accelerate the acquisition of goods and services?

Your answer is correct.

Your answer is incorrect.

The answer is: Acquisitions Program

The PSPC Acquisitions Program is divided in two branches—the Defence and Marine Procurement Branch and the Procurement Branch. Each branch handles the acquisition of specific groups of goods, services, and construction services. Purchases range from office supplies to military vessels to security systems, and everything in between.

Question  — Select the correct answer.

Who is responsible for managing the procurement process and contract management functions?

Your answer is correct.

Your answer is incorrect.

The answer is: Contracting authority (contracting officer)

The contracting authority is responsible for supporting the objectives of the senior designated official and taking into consideration the insights of the technical authorities, and managing the procurement process and contract management functions.
