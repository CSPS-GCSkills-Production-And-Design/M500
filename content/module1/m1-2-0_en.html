Who are the stakeholders in the procurement process?

Your role as a contracting officer is to plan and acquire goods, services, and construction services, to support the delivery of government programs that provide best value to Canadians and Government of Canada. This may entail:

  • preparing bid solicitation packages;
  • evaluating financial proposals;
  • managing the bid evaluation process;
  • selecting suppliers;
  • negotiating procurement agreements; and
  • formalizing procurement agreements.

While fulfilling this role, you will deal with the following key stakeholders.

Stakeholders in the procurement process

Contracting officer

Clients

Clients are responsible for defining their requirements and evaluation criteria, as well as identifying the total life-cycle cost of the procurement to meet their operational needs.

Bidders

A bidder or an offeror or a supplier can be a person or an entity submitting a bid or an offer or an arrangement as a result of a solicitation to perform a contract for goods, services, or both.

Contractors

Contractors (also known as suppliers or vendors) are responsible for carrying out the work and/or providing the goods and services in accordance with the terms and conditions of the contract.
