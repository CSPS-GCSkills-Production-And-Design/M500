Procurement Community

The Procurement community is made up of a wide range of specialists. Their roles cover a broad range of responsibilities, which may include:

  • designing, building, and negotiating procurement instruments for government-wide use;
  • acquiring goods and services to meet departmental program requirements;
  • providing policy interpretation and advice on existing and proposed policies, ensuring that policies, directives and best practices are understood and remain current and relevant; and
  • supporting the development of departmentsâ€™ procurement workforce.

Senior designated official (SDO)
*

The SDO leads the procurement initiatives within their respective department and aims to ensure all the objectives of the surrounding policy instruments, including socio-economic benefits, are met.

*Senior level position

Business owner
*

A business owner offers operational and technical expertise while collaborating with the contracting authority, including planning procurements that leverage socio-economic goals.

* Also known as: client, responsibility centre manager, technical/scientific authority, functional authority, project manager/authority/sponsor.

Contracting authority
*

A contracting authority supports the objectives of the SDO and takes into consideration the insights of the technical authorities, while managing the procurement process and contract management functions.

*Also known as: contracting officer, procurement officer, buyer and procurement specialist

Did you know?

The minister is the contracting authority and, in practice, the minister delegates his / her authority, in full or in part, to managers and contracting officers to contract on behalf of the department. As a CO, you are responsible for ensuring that you clearly understand what authority you have been sub delegated and that you work within your authority.
