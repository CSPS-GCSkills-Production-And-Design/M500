Policy on Investment Planning – Assets and Acquired Services

Departmental investment planning is the function of allocating and reallocating resources to new and existing assets and acquired services that are essential to program delivery. Departmental investment planning is a key element in achieving value for money and sound stewardship.

Under the investment planning lens, client needs are considered over a long-term horizon. Unless there is sustained investment in its asset base, a department’s ability to deliver on its mandate erodes over time. The investments that are proposed in such a plan are carefully chosen to maximize capabilities within the available resources that are allocated in a manner that clearly supports program outcomes and government priorities. Integrated investment planning is a critical activity that ensures the government’s ongoing ability to deliver the highest quality service to Canadians.

Investment Planning Context sets the stage for a department’s proposed investment decisions by outlining:

  • Who We Are and What We Do
  • The Assets We Use to Support Our Programs
  • Our Investment Requirements
  • Investment Funding available to the Department
  • How We Make Investment Decisions

Investment Decisions specify the investment vision of the department for the next five years and beyond by describing:

  • How We Will Invest Our Resources in the Next Five Fiscal Years
  • How We Will Address Risks related to the Integrated Investment Planning
  • How We Plan to Measure Our Performance Relative to the Integrated Investment Planning
  • How We Will Invest Our Resources beyond the Five Fiscal Years.

Policy Framework for Management of Assets and Acquired Services

The Policy Framework for the Management of Assets and Acquired Services and its associated policy instruments set the direction for the management of assets and acquired services to ensure that the conduct of these activities provides value for money and demonstrates sound stewardship in program delivery. The policy framework establishes the principles that should govern the work of the procurement, materiel management, and real property communities; these principles are in keeping with the Management Accountability Framework and are grounded in the Values and Ethics Code for the Public Sector.

The policy framework centres on the characteristics of an effective departmental management framework and the broad principles that should guide decision making rather than provide prescriptive, transaction-focused direction.

Deputy heads are accountable to their respective ministers and to Treasury Board for the management of departmental assets and acquired services. They are responsible for implementing an effective management framework, including departmental procedures, processes and systems that demonstrate how the department is managing based on the principles of procurement best practices.

The policy framework also identifies the key statutory, regulatory and policy instruments such as the Financial Administration Act, the Government Contracts Regulations, and the Common Services Policy that form the basis for the government’s management practices and controls.

Contracting Policy

The Contracting Policy addresses key applicable statutes and regulations, includes general policy and procedural requirements, and provides rules for bidding and selection, contract awarding, definitions for significant terms, and annual reporting requirements for contracting activities. It also deals with specific issues associated with goods, services and construction contracts, and contract administration.

Contracting officers should be aware of a number of key provisions specifically addressed in this policy, focusing on the following topics:

  • Acceptable Bonding Companies (Appendix L)
  • Advance Contract Award Notices (Articles 10.7.13–10.7.17)
  • Bidding and Selection (Article 10)
  • Conflict of Interest (Articles 16.11.5–16.11.7)
  • Contract Administration (Article 12)
  • Contract Award (Article 11)
  • Contract Splitting (Article 11.2.7)
  • Contracting for Advertising and Public Opinion Research Services (Article 16.13)
  • Contracting for Legal Services (Article 4.1.10)
  • Contracting with Former Public Servants in Receipt of a Pension or a Lump Sum Payment (Article 16.8)
  • Criminal Code (Article 12.5.1)
  • Employer-Employee Relationships (Article 16.3)
  • Federal Contractors’ Program for Employment Equity (Appendix D)
  • Government Security (Articles 11.3 and 12.5.2)
  • Lobbyists and Contracting (Appendix M)
  • Official Languages (Article 8.5 and Appendix F)
  • Temporary Help Services (Appendix I)
