Introduction

As previously stated, you will play the part of a contracting officer having to deal with different procurement scenarios.

First, however, it is important to understand the following legal and policy framework surrounding procurement and its impact on all decisions made by departments and their procurement personnel.

Select each element to learn more.

This pyramid features many of the key instruments of the procurement legal and policy framework, but not all of them.

Select each tab to view the description of some of these instruments

Act

Financial Administration Act

Provides the legal framework for the collection and expenditure of public funds, including the contracting practices of PSPC and its clients.

Regulation

Government Contract Regulations

Contains conditions of contract entry, and bid and contract security.

Agreement

North American Free Trade Agreement

Contains procurement provisions that focus on achieving greater competition for, and transparency in, government procurement, eliminating the protection of domestic products or suppliers or discrimination among foreign products or suppliers.

Policy

Treasury Board Contracting Policy

Explains the requirements to acquire goods and services and to carry out construction in a manner that enhances access, competition and fairness and results in best value.
