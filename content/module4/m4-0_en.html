Module : 4. Legal and Policy Framework
Module : 3. Delegation of Authority

Overview

Procurement in the Government of Canada is legislated and guided by a number of national and international trade agreements, statutes and regulations, as well as policies, directives, and guidelines established by Treasury Board (TB), the Treasury Board Secretariat (TBS), and Public Services and Procurement Canada (PSPC). In addition, certain departments such as Crown-Indigenous Relations and Northern Affairs Canada (CIRNAC), and Innovation, Science and Economic Development Canada, are responsible for programs that have a direct impact on procurement.

In order to properly exercise their duties, and provide sound advice to clients, federal contracting officers must have an in depth knowledge of the legal framework that guides government procurement.

Upon completion of this learning activity, you will be able to:

  • Identify key trade agreements, statutes and regulations, policies, directives, and guidelines that guide government procurement
  • Explain how key trade agreements, statutes and regulations, policies, directives, and guidelines impact government procurement
  • Apply the appropriate trade agreements, statutes and regulations, policies, directives, and guidelines to various government procurement scenarios

In this module, you will learn about the various trade agreements, statutes and regulations, policies, directives, and guidelines relevant to procurement using a “day-in-the-life” format. You will play the role of a contracting officer who is presented with various procurement scenarios and you will have to answer questions and/or offer an opinion. The appropriate and applicable instruments will then be presented along with an explanation of their impact on the particular situation.

This module will take approximately 2.5 to 3 hours to complete.
