Government Contracts and Real Property Leases in the Nunavut Settlement Area

The Treasury Board Directive on Government Contracts, Including Real Property Leases, in the Nunavut Settlement Area aims to increase participation by Inuit firms in business opportunities in the Nunavut Settlement Area (NSA), improve capacity of Inuit firms to compete for government contracts, and ensure employment of Inuit at a representative level in the NSA workforce.

The Directive requires deputy heads to provide in the NSA an annual contracting plan to the minister responsible for Indigenous Services Canada (ISC). Quarterly and annual reports of contracting activities are to be submitted to ISC (aadnc.nunavutprocurement-approvisionnementaununavut.aandc@canada.ca) and copy TBS (Nunavut@tbs-sct.gc.ca). More specifically, the reports collect data on government contracts valued at over $10,000 and real property leases valued at over $100,000 concluded in the NSA.

    The departments and agencies must follow the mandatory procedure on reporting provided in Appendix D of the Directive. The information collected includes, but is not limited to:

    • reference number
    • value of government contract or real property lease
    • vendor or lessor classification
    • type of government contract or real property lease
    • whether Inuit benefit criteria are included in the solicitation
    • value of Inuit benefits
    • description of benefits provided to Inuit firms and Inuit
