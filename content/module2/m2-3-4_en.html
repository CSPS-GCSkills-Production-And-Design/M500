Procurement Strategy for Aboriginal Business (PSAB)

The PSAB initiative aims to increase federal contracting opportunities and access to the federal procurement process for Aboriginal businesses. The PSAB is a national initiative led by Crown-Indigenous Relations and Northern Affairs Canada (CIRNAC). All federal government departments and agencies participate in this initiative. As such, departments and agencies with annual procurements of more than $1 million in total are required to develop multi-year performance objectives in the following areas, on a calendar-year basis:

  • estimated number of contract awards to Aboriginal business
  • estimated dollar value of contract awards to Aboriginal business
  • estimated representation of Aboriginal business in supplier development activities
  • estimated representation of Aboriginal business in supplier inventories

The departments and agencies must then report, the number and value of contracts awarded to Aboriginal businesses, quarterly and annually, to Public Service and Procurement Canada (PSPC), demonstrating how this activity measures up to the quantitative performance objectives. CIRNAC later verifies this data to ensure it is accurately captured; these results are then approved by TBS. Following approval, CIRNAC produces an annual PSAB report. Contracting officers must ensure the accuracy of reporting on procurements subject to a PSAB set aside.

PSAB uses a number of key elements to reach program objectives.

Mandatory Set‑Asides

Set–asides are mandatory when federal contracts for goods and services are worth more than $5,000 and delivered to an area, community or group in which Aboriginal people comprise at least 80% of the population.

Voluntary Set-Asides

Federal departments and agencies may choose to voluntarily set aside procurement opportunities for Aboriginal-owned businesses when operational requirements, best value, prudence, probity, and sound contracting management can be assured and where Aboriginal capacity exists.

Joint Ventures/Partnerships

The PSAB encourages joint ventures and partnerships to allow Aboriginal and non-Aboriginal partnerships to bid on opportunities that have been set aside for Aboriginal businesses. This is to help Aboriginal businesses build their capacity and benefit from knowledge and competency transfers.

Use of Aboriginal Criteria

In awarding contracts, departments are encouraged to request Aboriginal sub-contracting plans, either as a mandatory requirement or rated evaluation criteria.

This is not permitted for procurements subject to the international trade agreements.

Indigenous Business Directory

Crown-Indigenous Relations and Northern Affairs Canada (CIRNAC), in collaboration with Innovation, Science and Economic Development Canada, maintains an online Indigenous Business Directory that streamlines and simplifies the Procurement Strategy for Aboriginal Business (PSAB) registration process. Federal government contracting officers should be aware of the companies that are registered under the PSAB or the Ontario Aboriginal Business Directory.

These elements have proven to be very effective not only in helping Aboriginal businesses compete for and win federal contracts, but also in developing and building Aboriginal business capacity and expertise.
