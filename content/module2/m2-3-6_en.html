Ownership of Intellectual Property

Contracting authorities are required to gather information on the ownership of intellectual property (IP) and potential commercial exploitation in Crown procurement contracts exceeding $25,000. Although only numbers are reported, contracting authorities should always record the rationale for their ownership and commercial exploitation decisions in the contract file to facilitate future audits.

The data on IP ownership and potential commercial exploitation from the departmental systems are provided annually to Public Services and Procurement Canada (PSPC) which includes the number of times the Crown retained the ownership of the IP and the exceptions used to justify it. PSPC is responsible for providing this information to Innovation, Science and Economic Development Canada (ISED) and Treasury Board of Canada Secretariat (TBS).

Note: By default, the Contractor is to own the Foreground IP arising under Crown procurement contracts, unless the Crown claims one of the exceptions listed in Appendix A – Exceptions to Contractor Ownership and Treasury Board Exemption of the Policy on Title to Intellectual Property Arising Under Crown Procurement Contracts applies.
