Background

Parliament has sought to ensure that procurement is conducted in an open, fair and transparent manner and, wherever possible, in a way that maximizes competition, through provisions in:

  • The Financial Administration Act;
  • The Government Contracts Regulations; and
  • Canada's national and international trade agreements.

On December 12, 2006, the Federal Accountability Act was granted Royal Assent. Through this Act and an accompanying Action Plan, the Government of Canada brought forward specific measures to help strengthen accountability and increase transparency and oversight in government operations. For contracting officers, the Federal Accountability Act and the accompanying action plan contained a number of provisions that affect how they do their work. Department of Public Works and Government Services Act

The appointment and mandate of a Procurement Ombudsman, resulting in amendments to the existing Department of Public Works and Government Services Act.

Code of Conduct for Procurement

The introduction of a Code of Conduct for Procurement consolidating the existing suite of conflict-of-interest and anti-corruption policies for both suppliers and public-service employees. The Code aids in fulfilling commitments to reform procurement, ensuring greater transparency, accountability, and the highest standards of ethical conduct.

Office of Small and Medium Enterprises

More resources and greater regional presence for the Office of Small and Medium Enterprises (OSME), to help these businesses maintain access to government business opportunities and to ensure they are treated fairly.

Financial Administration Act

The amendment of the Financial Administration Act introduces the following:

  • a commitment to fairness, openness and transparency in the bidding process
  • regulation-making power to consider including certain clauses in contracts related to:
    • prohibiting contingency fees, and corruption and collusion in the bidding process
    • declarations by bidders regarding specific criminal offences
Criminal Code

The amendment of the Criminal Code making fraud with respect to public money an indictable offence and persons convicted of those offences ineligible for employment by the Crown.
