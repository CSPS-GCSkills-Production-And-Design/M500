Modern Treaties (Comprehensive Land Claims Agreements)

Comprehensive Land Claims Agreements (CLCAs) are modern treaties that are based on the concept of continued Aboriginal rights and title to lands that have been traditionally used and occupied, by an Aboriginal group, which have not been dealt with by treaty or other legal means.

Pursuant to modern treaties (CLCAs), the deputy heads of government departments must report to the deputy head of Crown-Indigenous Relations and Northern Affairs Canada (CIRNAC) on contracts entered into by their departments, and contracts entered into by Public Services and Procurement Canada (PSPC) on behalf of their departments, within 45 calendar days after the end of the quarter.

To learn more about the elements included in these reports, refer to the Contracting Policy, Section 5.1.4.1.

Note that if no contracting has occurred in a modern treaty (CLCA) area, deputy heads must still provide a statement to CIRNAC at the end of each quarter.

Upon receipt of these reports from deputy heads, the deputy head of CIRNAC must prepare and disclose, on a public Government of Canada website, a quarterly report as well as an annual report on contracts for each area covered by a  modern treaty (CLCA).

It is the responsibility of the contracting officers to capture this information in their department's Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) system.
