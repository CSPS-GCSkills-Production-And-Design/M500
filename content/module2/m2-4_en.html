Question 5 — Select the correct answer.

When a bid solicitation is national in scope, all regular and standardized documents must be provided in both official languages.

Your answer is correct.

Your answer is incorrect.

The answer is: True.

When bid solicitations are national in scope or originate from an office having the obligation to serve the public in both official languages, all regular or standardized documents must be provided in both official languages. This requirement also applies to public notices such as Notices of Proposed Procurement, terms and conditions, basic forms, bid solicitations, standards, purchase descriptions, and contracts.

When the bidding is not national in scope or when an office of a federal institution does not have obligations under the Act and Regulations, the contracting documents may be prepared only in the official language of the majority of the population concerned.

Where non standardized documents, such as specifications, are used, the federal institution initiating the requirement is responsible for determining if these documents must be available in both official languages. Such documents may be provided in only one official language when the institution determines and can substantiate, based on relevant information regarding their public and the marketplace, that they will be requested in that language only.

Question 5 — Select the correct answer.

The responsibility of reviewing complaints with respect to the awarding of a contract for the acquisition of goods below $26,400 and services below $105,700 lies with the:

Your answer is correct.

Your answer is incorrect.

The answer is: Procurement Ombudsman.

The Procurement Ombudsman is mandated to:

  • Review complaints with respect to the awarding of a contract for the acquisition of goods
    below $26,400 and services below $105,700;
  • Review complaints with respect to the administration of a contract, regardless of its dollar value;
  • Review the practices of departments for acquiring goods and services and make recommendations where necessary to improve those practices;
  • Ensure that an alternative dispute-resolution process is provided, if both parties agree to participate; and
  • Provide an alternative dispute-resolution service that:
    • helps the parties to the contract understand each other’s views;
    • focuses on resolving the dispute in a quick, positive and respectful manner;
    • costs less and is less time-consuming than court proceedings;
    • allows the parties full control over outcomes (no decision is “imposed”); and
    • leads to a mutual agreement on a legally binding settlement.

Question 5 — Select the correct answer.

During the procurement process, when a supplier needs to address an issue or wants to raise a complaint, they will normally first contact the:

Your answer is correct.

Your answer is incorrect.

The answer is: Contracting officer in the relevant department or agency.

Normally, the contracting officer is contacted first to discuss a supplier’s issue or concern. If the issue or complaint cannot be resolved at the department/agency level, the supplier can contact one of the following procurement complaint bodies, depending on the nature of the issue:

  • Competition Bureau
  • Canadian International Trade Tribunal
  • Office of the Procurement Ombudsman
  • Contracting Authorities
  • Officers of Parliament
