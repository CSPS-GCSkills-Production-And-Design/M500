Overview

In Canada, public procurement is guided and legislated by a number of national and international trade agreements, acts and regulations, as well as policies, directives, standards, and guidelines provided by Treasury Board of Canada Secretariat (TBS).

Government departments, boards, and agencies are accountable for the integrity of their procurements. They must ensure that all procurement projects are processed openly, with transparency and fairness.

The procurement challenge and oversight function is a key component of the broader set of management controls that are used to ensure the sound management of government procurement.

At the end of this learning module, you will be able to:

  • Identify the various means of oversight and reporting requirements that strengthen government accountability in procurement projects

This module will take approximately 40 to 50 minutes to complete.
